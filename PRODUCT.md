# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
New merchants visiting the site for the first time. They run physical businesses (cafes, market stalls, retail counters) in Indonesia. They are evaluating whether Pulse is worth trusting and easy enough to set up. Their primary action on arrival is to create an account or sign in to an existing one.

## Product Purpose
Pulse is a Solana-native programmable settlement rail for real-world payments. An NFC sticker at a merchant counter triggers a checkout PWA; the customer taps, approves payment in their Solana wallet, and funds settle with split-payment logic in under 2 seconds. The landing page must earn the trust of a skeptical merchant, demonstrate the product working, and route them to registration without friction.

## Brand Personality
Precise, fast, trustworthy. Not generic crypto. Not decorative fintech.

## Anti-references
Generic "neon on black" crypto landing pages. SaaS landing pages built around big hero metrics (large number, small label, gradient accent). Identical icon-heading-text card grids. Pages that explain blockchain mechanics to merchants who do not care about them. Anything that feels like a design template with Solana colors applied on top.

## Design Principles
Show, don't explain: the live payment terminal demo does more work than any paragraph of copy.
Trust through specificity: amounts, settlement times, and wallet ownership are stated exactly, not abstracted.
Fast to the CTA: a new merchant must reach a register button within the first viewport.
Merchant-first language: operational clarity over technology marketing.
Dark precision: the dark interface signals a professional tool, not a decorative choice.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG AA minimum. All text on dark backgrounds must meet contrast thresholds. Touch targets minimum 44px. Status communicated via text and color together, never color alone. Reduced motion preference respected via prefers-reduced-motion.
